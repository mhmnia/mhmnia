§ 01 — About

A geophysicist working at the intersection of satellite remote sensing and volcanology.

I am a PhD researcher at the University of Hong Kong, advised by Dr. Pablo J. González (CSIC, Spain) and Prof. Alex Webb (FU Berlin). My research uses Interferometric Synthetic Aperture Radar (InSAR) to detect, quantify, and interpret subtle ground deformation — from awakening volcanoes to creeping salt diapirs.

I develop new processing methods to push InSAR sensitivity beyond conventional limits, with a particular focus on atmospheric correction for small, steep volcanic edifices where traditional techniques fall short. I combine these geodetic observations with numerical modeling, petrological constraints, and increasingly machine learning, to build a more complete picture of subsurface processes.

My recent work on Taftan volcano (Iran) — which uncovered the first clear unrest signal at a long-dormant volcano — received global media coverage in outlets including The Independent, New York Post, and Daily Mail.

§ 03 — Selected publications

Recent work, in peer-reviewed journals.

— 01
Geophysical Research Letters · 2025

Spontaneous transient summit uplift at Taftan volcano (Makran subduction arc) imaged using an InSAR common-mode filtering method Media

M. Mohammadnia, M. W. Yip, A. A. G. Webb, P. J. González

First clear documentation of unrest at Taftan, a long-dormant volcano in southeastern Iran. Using a novel common-mode filtering technique applied to Sentinel-1 time series, we resolve a 10-month summit uplift episode (~9 cm) between July 2023 and May 2024, sourced ~500 m beneath the summit. The signal — triggerless and uncorrelated with rainfall or seismicity — points to hydrothermal pressurization, reframing Taftan as a hidden hazard along the Makran arc.

— 02
Bulletin of Volcanology · 2026

Satellite surface deformation survey reveals evidence for active magmatic processes at the Tibesti (Africa) continental intraplate volcanoes

M. Mohammadnia, T. Boulesteix, A. A. G. Webb, P. J. González

The first satellite-based deformation survey across the Tibesti volcanic province in the central Sahara of Chad — one of Earth's least-studied intraplate magmatic systems. Sentinel-1 time series reveal persistent subsidence at the Toussidé–Yirrigué complex (−2.2 mm/yr) and Soborom geothermal field (−8.5 mm/yr), interpreted via thermodynamic and mechanical modeling as long-term cooling of mid-crustal magma reservoirs emplaced during the Holocene.

— 03
Geophysical Journal International · 2025

Surface deformation dynamics of Sierra Negra's 2018 eruption: insights from InSAR, optical flow and pixel offset tracking

M. Mohammadnia, S. Vajedian, Z. Mousavi, M. Aflaki

A multi-method study of the 2018 Sierra Negra eruption (Galápagos) combining InSAR, optical flow, and pixel offset tracking on consecutive 6-day Sentinel-1 pairs. The integrated workflow captures the full eruption cycle — including caldera subsidence at metric rates — and estimates an underground magma transfer rate of ~60 m/hr, consistent with seismically inferred magma migration through a sub-horizontal dike.

— 04
Tectonophysics · 2021

InSAR constraints on the active deformation of salt diapirs in the Kalut basin, Central Iran

M. Mohammadnia, M. Najafi, Z. Mousavi

A comprehensive InSAR study of eight salt diapirs in the Kalut basin of central Iran — one of the most active exposed diapiric systems on Earth. Vertical growth rates reach 8 mm/yr at salt stocks and large walls, with horizontal flow of 1–2 mm/yr along flanks. The work classifies diapir kinematics (stocks, walls, massifs, welds) and links rising structures to ephemeral fluvial and evaporitic depocenters accumulating up to 10 mm/yr of sediment.
